About Me

I am a Associate Prof. with School of Computer Science and Technology, Zhejiang University of Technology. Meanwhile, I am currently a Postdoc Research Fellow working with Prof. Hong Zhang in the R/V lab at University of Alberta, CA. Before that, I received my Ph.D degree from Department of Mechanical and Biomedical in City University of Hong Kong in 2015, supervised under Prof. Y.F. Li

My current research focuses on creating algorithms that allow robots to intelligently and friendly interact with people, which can be applied to robots that work in homes, factories, and labs. I am now specifically interested in continuous action recognition based on key actionlet detection. I draw ideas from dierential geometry, pattern recognition, optimization, and deep learning to develop algorithms that enable robots detect and recognize actions and activities from continuous videos in a human-like way. I am also seek to develop approaches in various aspects, including feature extraction, shape description, and shape matching.
